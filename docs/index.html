Jesus Christ is the central figure of Christianity and is believed by Christians to be the Son of God and the Savior of the world. His life, teachings, death, and resurrection are the foundation of the Christian faith.
Get to know Jesus
His Birth and Early Life- Jesus was born in Bethlehem to the Virgin Mary, fulfilling Old Testament prophecies about the coming of the Messiah (Matthew 1:18-25, Luke 2:1-20). His birth is celebrated as Christmas. He grew up in Nazareth and worked as a carpenter before beginning His ministry.
His Teachings and Miracles-Around the age of 30, Jesus began His public ministry, preaching about God’s Kingdom, love, forgiveness, and salvation. He performed many miracles, such as healing the sick, raising the dead, feeding thousands, calming storms, and casting out demons (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John). He taught using parables (short stories with deep spiritual meanings), like the Parable of the Good Samaritan and the Parable of the Prodigal Son.
His Death and Sacrifice-Jesus was falsely accused by religious leaders and sentenced to death by crucifixion under the Roman governor Pontius Pilate. His crucifixion took place on Good Friday, and He died to pay for the sins of humanity (Isaiah 53:5, John 19:30). He was buried in a tomb, and His followers thought all hope was lost.
His Resurrection and Victory-On the third day (Easter Sunday), Jesus rose from the dead, proving His power over sin and death (Luke 24:6-7). He appeared to His disciples and many others before ascending to heaven, promising to return one day (Acts 1:9-11).
His Message and Salvation-Jesus offers forgiveness, grace, and eternal life to all who believe in Him (John 3:16). His death and resurrection mean that anyone who accepts Him as Lord and Savior will be saved (Romans 10:9). He taught that the greatest commandment is to love God and love others (Matthew 22:37-39).
His Second Coming-The Bible teaches that Jesus will return one day to establish His kingdom and judge the world (Revelation 22:12).
